# MotionSwipe
MotionSwipe is a gesture-based navigation system that enables users to swipe on their phone screen without physical contact. 


Abstract: Almost everyone interacts with smartphones daily, whether it be for
communication, work, or entertainment. Traditional touch-based navigation can
sometimes be limited, especially in a situation where the user cannot use his hands
freely. MotionSwipe is a gesture-based navigation system that enables users to swipe
on their phone screen without physical contact. By using the smart phone camera,
MotionSwipe can detect hand movements to simulate swiping gestures. This allows for
the user to scroll through content, switch apps, and navigate through menus without the
use of physical contact. The accelerometer can further detect gestures and distinguish
between unintended phone movements. MotionSwipe enhances accessibility and
usability, providing an intuitive and touch-free way to interact with smartphones.
Scenario: This idea helps those who want a more convenient and hands-free way to
navigate through their smartphones. People are sometimes put into situations where
they cannot use their hands freely to touch their phones, such as cooking, working out,
or wearing gloves in cold weather. Anytime one’s hands are occupied, MotionSwipe can
become very useful.
